Perfect money-payment processor

What is Perfect Money?

Perfect Money is an e-payment system (online payment), similar to Payeer, PayPal, Skrill and so on. You can use it to send and receive money online. It is an online payment processor for various money transactions over the internet. Perfect Money was founded in 2007, is a registered company in Panama, but based in Zurich, Switzerland. With the Perfect Money payment system, the user can choose any of the available multi-currency accounts (i.e. Dollars, Euros and Gold). Transferring money is very simple. Account holders can easily use e-vouchers or any other approved method to deposit money into their PM accounts. Perfect Money is a great payment system, but it is relatively new and not very popular. Honestly, some people have trouble deciding whether to trust this "great new platform" or not.

What Types of Accounts Are Available?

There are 3 accounts available to you: In the bill and some number - it's a bill in US dollars An invoice and a number - it is an invoice in euros G account and some number - it's an account in gold. Regardless of the type of your account, you can exchange money directly on your account at the current exchange rate on the international market.

What Are The Types Of Accounts At PM?

There are two types of accounts - personal and business (Personal and Business) Personal account is for individual users, and business for legal entities. There are also subtypes: premium account and partner-level account. The premium account fee for each bank transfer is only 2 percent; and annual interest rates are around 8 percent.

Is Perfect Money Safe?

Unlike Liberty Reserve, accounts on Perfect Money are less phishing targets because they are less popular. Perfect Money Corporation has raised security barriers that prevent fraudsters from making illegal re-withdrawals and transactions using a legitimate account. The company succeeded in three phases: First, one of the ways the company does this is through an IP address protection system. During the registration of the Perfect Money account, the user's IP address is recorded. Therefore, if that user tries to access their account from another IP address, they must enter a specific PIN code. Second, if configured, Perfect Money often asks for authentication via SMS every time a new IP address tries to access your account. This is a great way to protect yourself from hacking. Third, since hackers usually have a way to access accounts, Perfect Money issues a code card that has several codes; so each time a transaction is started, the code must be filled in for the transaction to pass.
